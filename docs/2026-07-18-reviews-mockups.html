Row 3 is the case worth designing for. The reported content is an owner's reply, not a diner's review. Owners behaving badly in public is a real moderation category and the queue has to hold both — which is why replies carry their own status column and reports carry a nullable reply_id.

Settled: owners are not exempt from the filter. Reply text runs through the exact same moderation check as review text, at the same thresholds, with the same blocking behavior and the same fail-closed posture. Paying to be on the platform doesn't buy a lower bar for conduct — and an owner swearing at a diner in public does more damage to Trust Halal than the review that provoked it.

Decisions these mockups surface

Settled in review

✓ No verifier badge on reviews. Every review carries equal weight; verifier standing stays in the verification history.
✓ Owner mobile nav: four tabs. Reviews reached from a badged Home card. Desktop keeps the fifth nav link.
✓ Owner replies pass the same profanity filter as reviews. Same thresholds, same blocking, same fail-closed posture. No exemption.
✓ Removal reasons are sent to the author, written to them rather than as an internal note.

Settled in round two

✓ Admins never open disputes. Removed from A2. Filing an accusation on a diner's behalf would put Trust Halal's name on their claim — that record stays the consumer's own.
✓ Email confirmation required before posting. Guests can't review; neither can an unconfirmed account. Phone verification rejected — see the plan for why.
✓ Sort: Most recent / Highest first / Lowest first. Drawn in C1.

New prerequisite this surfaced

Email verification does not exist in the platform today. Signup creates the account and logs you straight in — the signup docstring says so explicitly. Requiring a confirmed address before reviewing means building email verification in auth first: a nullable users.email_verified_at, a third purpose on the existing invite-token table, a template pair, and two endpoints. The machinery is already there and was written with this in mind, but it's a real step-zero that lands before any review code.
