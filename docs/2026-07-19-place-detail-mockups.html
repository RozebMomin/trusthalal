The verdict never leaves the screen. On desktop the answer stays pinned while photos, reviews and hours scroll past it — which is the actual fix for "there's a lot of info". Nothing is removed; it stops competing for the same vertical space.
Cost, honestly: C needs a real layout change to place-detail-client.tsx (grid + sticky) and every section restyled to work at 320px and at full width. B is mostly reordering and re-weighting components that already exist. C contains B — the phone layout is identical — so B first and C later is a valid path, not wasted work.

What changed, and why

One verdict instead of four statements. "Halal certified", "certificate on file", "Certified by HMS" and "last verified 7 days ago" are one claim with its provenance. Collapsing them frees the top of the page for the answer and puts the evidence one tap behind it.

Google's rating leaves the hero. It's still shown, still labelled, still next to yours — but it stops being the first number a visitor reads on a page about your verification. Right now the loudest fact on a Trust Halal profile is somebody else's score.

The slaughter grid only shows what's served. Two "Not served" tiles were half the widget. They become one quiet line underneath.

Dead sign-in rows become one footer line. Two full-width cards that do nothing for signed-out visitors — the overwhelming majority of first-time traffic — collapse into a single line that also carries the owner-claim CTA.

"No halal info yet" → "Halal info wanted". Identical fact, opposite posture. One advertises a hole; the other asks for help and hints that the catalogue is something people build.
