# Brand canon

> **What this is:** the durable, canonical statement of what Trust Halal *is* and how we talk about it.
> Change it deliberately — marketing copy, store listings, and the app should not contradict what's here.
> **What this is NOT:** working content. Caption drafts, the content calendar, and image/video assets
> live in the marketing workspace (Notion / Drive), not in the repo.

## One line

The halal restaurant finder that shows the **evidence** behind every claim — so you decide with your own eyes, not blind faith.

## Positioning

For Muslim diners tired of guessing whether "halal" really means halal, Trust Halal is the only finder that shows the *proof* behind every claim — a verified visit, a certificate on file, or plainly-labeled self-attestation — so you decide with your own eyes, not blind faith.

## Campaign concept

**Proof, not promises.**

The emotional engine is the contrast between *guessing* and *knowing*. Content makes the tier badge the hero, because that badge is the product's promise: we never dress up the owner's word as our endorsement.

Backup concept names, if ever needed: "Halal, with receipts" · "Know before you go." (Not in use — retained as options.)

## The two audiences (same idea, two angles)

- **Diners — the relief angle.** No more interrogating a server, no more avoiding a place because you're unsure. Trust Halal removes the guesswork.
- **Owners — the credibility angle.** "Your halal is real; prove it and get found." Leans on *free for restaurants, forever* and *independent of any certifying body*.

## Messaging pillars

1. **Evidence over faith** — every claim has a receipt (the three tiers).
2. **Radically honest** — we say so plainly when we *don't* know. Even our own paid meals are disclosed.
3. **Independent & free** — not owned by any certifying body; free for restaurants, forever.
4. **Community-built** — real verifier visits, real diners.

## Verification tiers — canonical colour rules

These mirror the product. Marketing must match them exactly; a mismatch undercuts the entire pitch.

| Tier | Colour | Means |
|---|---|---|
| Trust Halal Verified | **Emerald** | A human verifier visited in person and filed a report. |
| Certificate on File | **Amber** | A real, unexpired certificate is on file. |
| Self-Attested | **Slate** — *never green* | The owner's own word; nobody checked, and we say so. |

**Hard rule:** emerald/green is reserved for *Verified* only. Self-Attested is never shown in green, even decoratively.

## The three trust commitments (brand-site "trust strip")

- Independent of any certifying body
- Free for restaurants, forever
- Every paid meal disclosed
