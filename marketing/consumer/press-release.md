## Standard press release — for Muslim media, food bloggers, local press

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Trust Halal Launches Verified Directory of Halal Restaurants**

**The community-built platform aims to end "is this actually halal?" with verified listings that include supplier, slaughter method, and certification on file.**

[CITY, DATE] — Trust Halal, a new directory of verified halal restaurants, launched today at **halalfoodnearme.com**. The platform addresses a long-standing pain point for halal-conscious diners: scattered, unverifiable claims about which restaurants are actually halal and what "halal" means at each one.

Restaurants apply to be verified through Trust Halal's owner portal. The verification process captures four areas: menu posture (fully halal, halal options on a mixed menu, separate kitchens), per-product meat sourcing (supplier name and slaughter method for each meat), certification on file (uploaded from any recognized body), and alcohol policy. Verified listings carry a "Trust Halal Verified" badge and a public halal profile that diners can read and filter against.

"Every halal-conscious diner has a story about being told a place is halal and finding out it wasn't," said [Founder Name], [title] at Trust Halal. "The information has been scattered across Yelp reviews, Google listings, and group chats. We wanted to put it in one place and check it."

Trust Halal offers three verification tiers — Self-attested, Certificate on file, and Trust Halal Verified (which requires an in-person visit by a community verifier in the last 12 months). All three are visible by default in search; diners can filter to top tier only if they prefer.

The platform is Muslim-led and free for restaurants. It launched today in [Launch City] with plans to expand based on community waitlists.

**About Trust Halal**

Trust Halal is a Muslim-led, community-built platform that verifies halal restaurants and surfaces what verification means in plain terms. The consumer-facing brand is Halal Food Near Me (halalfoodnearme.com). Trust Halal is independent of any specific certifying body and accepts certificates from any recognized halal authority.

## Press release notes

* **No exclamation points** in the headline or body. Treat it as the news story, not a brochure.
* **Lead with the consumer pain**, not the platform's features. "Tired of asking" beats "Introducing the world's first."
* **A direct quote from the founder** in paragraph 3 makes it printable in places that want quotes — even if the journalist doesn't use the exact quote.
* **Founder anonymity / privacy:** if you'd rather not put your face in press, use a generic title ("Trust Halal team") and skip the headshot. Reduces the personality angle but keeps the news angle.
* **Local angle is your friend.** Make the press release feel local to whichever city you're pitching — drop the launch city in the headline, mention a couple of verified restaurants by name.
* **Embargo or "live now"?** Live now is fine for early-stage launches. Embargoes work when you have ~5 outlets ready to publish simultaneously — usually overkill at this stage.

## Don'ts

* Don't claim you're "the first" or "the only." Hard to verify and feels defensive.
* Don't compare to Zabihah or any other directory by name. Position positively.
* Don't list every verified restaurant in the press release — pick 2–3 of the most recognizable for color, mention the count, leave the rest as something the reader can discover.
* Don't lead the release with the brand voice / community framing. Lead with the news. The voice gets the spotlight later in the piece.
