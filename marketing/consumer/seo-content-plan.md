# SEO Content Plan

## The strategy

Most halal restaurant searches happen via Google. The searches are predictable and high-intent:

* `"halal restaurants near me"`
* `"halal pizza brooklyn"`
* `"best halal indian food atlanta"`
* `"is [restaurant name] halal"`
* `"halal restaurants in [city]"`
* `"zabihah chicken near me"`

You can rank for many of these by building **city + cuisine combination pages** that genuinely answer the search. Each page becomes a long-burn SEO asset that keeps drawing traffic for years.

The win condition isn't "rank #1 for halal restaurants" — that's table-stakes-impossible. The win condition is ranking for the long tail of `halal [cuisine] [city]` combinations where there's real demand but weak existing content.

Below: 10 starter pages, each with a brief on title, H1, structure, and a sample paragraph. Write 1–2 per week to start. Don't try to write all 10 in a single sprint — Google rewards consistency, not bursts.

## Page 1 — Halal Restaurants in Brooklyn

**Target query:** "halal restaurants brooklyn"
**URL:** `/halal-restaurants-brooklyn`
**Title tag (≤60 chars):** Halal Restaurants in Brooklyn — Trust Halal
**Meta description (≤160 chars):** Verified halal restaurants in Brooklyn. See supplier, slaughter method, and certificate for every listing. Updated weekly.
**H1:** Verified Halal Restaurants in Brooklyn

**Structure:**
1. Intro paragraph (100–150 words): what verified means here, why this list is different.
2. The list itself: top 10–20 verified Brooklyn halal restaurants. Each has: name, neighborhood, cuisine, one-line "what's verified" detail, link to full listing.
3. Filter chips: by cuisine, by neighborhood, by trust tier.
4. "What we verified" section: brief explainer of menu posture, supplier, cert.
5. "Don't see a restaurant you trust?" — nomination form CTA.
6. Internal links: to cuisine sub-pages (halal pizza brooklyn, halal indian brooklyn, etc.).

**Sample intro paragraph:**

> Brooklyn has dozens of halal restaurants — but knowing which ones actually source halal meat, which ones have a certificate on file, and which ones operate from a shared kitchen has been a research project. This page lists the Brooklyn halal restaurants verified by Trust Halal. Every listing carries a public halal profile with the supplier name, slaughter method per meat, alcohol policy, and (when applicable) a downloadable halal certificate. No vague claims, no "halal options available." Updated weekly as more restaurants get verified.

## Page 2 — Halal Pizza in [City]

**Target query:** "halal pizza [city]"
**URL:** `/halal-pizza-[city-slug]`
**Title tag:** Halal Pizza in [City] — Verified Spots, Real Sourcing
**H1:** Halal Pizza in [City]

**Structure:**
1. Intro: halal pizza is one of the highest-asked-about cuisines; tell the reader why halal pizza is tricky (cheese rennet, pepperoni alternatives, shared ovens with non-halal items).
2. Verified halal pizza places in [city] — list with supplier details for the meat (turkey pepperoni, beef sausage, halal chicken, etc.) and cheese.
3. "What to ask if you're not sure" — the questions that a halal-conscious diner should ask any new pizza place.
4. Cross-link: other halal cuisines in this city, halal pizza in nearby cities.

**Sample paragraph:**

> Halal pizza is one of the trickier cuisines to verify — even when the pizzeria says "halal," the questions stack up: is the pepperoni turkey or beef, and where's it from? Is the rennet in the cheese halal? Is the oven shared with pork-topping pizzas? The verified halal pizza places in [city] listed below have answered those questions on their public listings. The supplier behind each meat topping, the slaughter method, and the shared-kitchen status are all available before you order.

**Target query:** "halal indian food [city]" / "halal pakistani food [city]"
**URL:** `/halal-indian-pakistani-[city-slug]`
**Title tag:** Halal Indian & Pakistani Restaurants in [City] — Trust Halal
**H1:** Verified Halal Indian and Pakistani Restaurants in [City]

**Structure:**
* Cover the cuisine duo together (they overlap heavily for diners).
* Lead with the heavy-hitters (most popular verified spots).
* Mention specific dishes — biryani, nihari, korma, kebab — that diners search for.

> Halal Indian and Pakistani restaurants in [city] range from quick biryani counters to family-style sit-down spots. The verified restaurants listed here have submitted their supplier details for chicken, beef, lamb, and goat — and many of them carry a current halal certificate. If you're looking for a specific dish (biryani, nihari, kebab, korma), each restaurant's profile lists their menu posture and the per-meat sourcing — so you can match the restaurant to what you actually want to order tonight.

## On-page SEO basics for every page

* H1 once per page; H2s for sections; H3s for sub-sections.
* Internal links to related pages — every cuisine page links to the city page and to the cross-cuisine pages in the same city.
* Schema.org JSON-LD: use `Restaurant` markup for individual listings (already done in the codebase), and `ItemList` for the category pages.
* Open Graph tags for social sharing — title, description, hero image.
* Page-load speed under 2.5s (already handled by Vercel + Next.js — just don't kill it with hero videos).
* Mobile-first design (already done).

## Off-page / link-building (later)

* **Mosque websites** — many mosques publish "halal eateries we recommend" lists. Reach out and offer to be a source. Naturally earned link.
* **MSA websites** — university Muslim Student Associations often publish similar resources.
* **Halal certifying bodies** — IFANCA, HFSAA, etc. — see if they'll link to your directory as a "places carrying our certification" listing. Mutually beneficial.
* **Halal food bloggers** — coverage from places like Halal Foodie, MuslimMatters Food section, and city-specific halal food bloggers builds organic backlinks.
* **Local news coverage** — see `press-release.md`.

## Cadence and tracking

* Write 1–2 SEO pages per week. Quality over quantity.
* Each page gets refreshed every 6 months — add new restaurants, freshen the intro paragraph.
* Track ranking weekly for your target queries. Tools: Ahrefs, Semrush, or just manually searching incognito.
* Google Search Console is free and good enough at this stage — set it up the day you launch the first SEO page.

## Don'ts

* Don't keyword-stuff. "Halal halal halal restaurant best halal halal" in the H1 is a 2008 strategy that now gets penalized.
* Don't publish thin "directory" pages that are just lists with no editorial intro. Google penalizes these.
* Don't AI-generate the page bodies and ship them as-is. They sound generic and rank poorly. AI-assisted editing is fine — fully AI-written copy is not.
* Don't try to rank for "halal food" as a single phrase. Pick long-tail combinations where you can actually win.
