# Owner Cold Outreach — Email Templates

Three variants for different scenarios. **Always personalize line 1 and the restaurant-specific detail in paragraph 2 before sending.** A generic blast reads as spam; one personal detail per email makes it land.

---

## Template A — The default cold email

**Subject:** A free verified listing for [Restaurant Name]

**Body:**

Hi [Owner first name, if known — otherwise "team at Restaurant Name"],

I've been ordering from [Restaurant Name] for a while ([one specific detail — the lamb biryani on Fridays, the kunafa, the breakfast haleem, whatever's actually true and specific]). The food is excellent.

I'm reaching out because I'm part of the team building **Trust Halal** — a verified directory of halal restaurants. The short version: most halal-conscious diners have been burned at some point by a "halal" place that turned out not to be, and the information is scattered across Yelp, Google, and group chats. We're trying to fix that by verifying restaurants ourselves and giving diners one place to search.

We'd love to verify [Restaurant Name]. Verification is free, takes about 15 minutes of your time, and includes:

* A public listing on **halalfoodnearme.com** showing your menu posture, supplier details, and any halal certificate on file
* A "Trust Halal Verified" badge for your window and your Instagram bio
* Direct control of your listing through your owner portal

If you're interested, you can apply at **owner.trusthalal.org** — takes maybe 10–15 minutes. Happy to walk you through it on a quick call if that's easier.

Either way, thank you for the food. Looking forward to keep coming back.

[Your name]
Trust Halal
hello@trusthalal.org

## Template B — Already-claiming-halal restaurant (warmer)

Use this when the restaurant ALREADY advertises as halal on Google/Yelp/their menu. The angle is: "you're already telling the world; let's make it verifiable."

**Subject:** Verifying [Restaurant Name]'s halal claim — interested?

**Body:**

Hi [Owner first name],

[Restaurant Name] already shows up as halal on Google and on your menu, which is great — but the community has no way to verify what that actually means. Is the chicken zabihah? What's the supplier? Is there alcohol in the kitchen?

I'm building **Trust Halal** — a directory of halal restaurants where the answers to those questions live on every listing, checked by us. The goal is that halal-conscious diners can find you without having to call the restaurant or hope a Yelp review mentioned the meat sourcing.

The verification is free and asks ~10 minutes of your time. The output is a public listing at halalfoodnearme.com plus a verified badge for your window and bio. The badge does the question-answering for you.

If it sounds useful: **owner.trusthalal.org** to apply, or reply to this and I'll send a direct link. Happy to jump on a quick call too.

Thanks for what you do.

[Your name]
Trust Halal

**Subject:** Quick question about [Restaurant Name]'s halal sourcing

**Body:**

A couple of friends mentioned that [Restaurant Name] sources halal meat, even though it's not advertised on the menu. (If that's wrong, apologies — feel free to ignore this.)

Thanks for considering it.

## Outreach hygiene

* **Use a real email address.** No noreply@. The reply path matters — half the value is the conversations it starts.
* **Hand-personalize line 1 and one specific detail in paragraph 2.** Even if it's something simple like "I came in last Thursday and the [dish] was great." If you can't think of one true detail, don't send the email.
* **Keep your sending volume reasonable.** 10–20 personalized emails a day is plenty. 100 generic blasts a day is a deliverability problem waiting to happen and reads as spam.
* **Track who you've emailed.** A simple Google Sheet with columns: Restaurant | Owner name | Email | Date sent | Reply? | Status. After 50 emails you'll see which restaurants are receptive and what objections come up.
* **Don't follow up more than once.** If no answer after the original + one follow-up, move on. They might come back later.
* **When someone says no:** thank them and ask if they'd be open to a touch-base in 6 months. About a third say yes to that.

## What to do when someone replies

**Positive reply ("yes, interested"):** Send the application link with a one-line "great to hear — here's the link, takes ~15 minutes, ping me if anything's confusing." Don't oversell once they've already said yes.

**Lukewarm reply ("tell me more"):** Send the 1-page sales sheet PDF (see `sales-sheet.md`). Offer to jump on a 15-minute call.

**Skeptical reply ("what's the catch / who's behind this / why's it free"):** Lead with transparency. "No catch — we're funded by [your funding source]. Verification stays free for restaurants in this phase. Long-term, we'll likely add paid features like featured placement, but the verified listing itself will always be free." Be honest about the business model.

**Hostile reply (rare):** Thank them for their time. Don't argue. Move on.

---

## A note on tone

These templates lean warm and direct. **Resist the urge to dress them up.** Real owners running busy restaurants don't want fluff; they want to know what you want from them and what they get out of it. The most effective version of this email is often shorter than the templates above, not longer. Trim ruthlessly.
