# Owner Social Post Templates

Copy + paste these into your owner portal's "Share" section. When a restaurant gets verified, send them this file (or post it as a Notion/Google Doc they can copy from).

Each template includes: an Instagram caption, a Twitter/X version, and a description of the recommended visual. The owner's restaurant name fills in `[Restaurant]`; the city fills in `[City]`.

## Use rules (give these to owners)

* **Personalize before posting.** Drop in the real supplier name, the real cert authority, the real city — not the bracket placeholders.
* **Tag @TrustHalal** (handle TBD once social channels are set up). When we see the post, we'll often share it to our own audience too — multiplies reach.
* **Don't fake what isn't true.** If you don't have a halal cert, use template 1 or 3, not template 2 (which talks about the supplier and would only work if true).
* **Story-first, feed-second.** Stories die after 24 hours but get more eyeballs. Post to stories AND save as a highlight.
* **Don't post all of these at once.** Spread them out over weeks. One per 2–4 weeks is plenty. Audiences tune out anything that looks like a campaign.

## Hashtag set (rotate, don't stuff)

Core: `#TrustHalal #HalalFoodNearMe`
City/locale: `#Halal[City]` (e.g. #HalalBrooklyn, #HalalDetroit)
Cuisine: pick 1–2 (`#HalalDesi #HalalMediterranean #HalalSomali` etc.)
Community: `#MuslimEats #HalalEats` (use sparingly; broad and noisy)

Don't pile on 30 hashtags. Five well-chosen ones beat thirty random ones.
