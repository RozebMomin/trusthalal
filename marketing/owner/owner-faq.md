## The basics

### What is Trust Halal?

Trust Halal is a verified directory of halal restaurants. Halal-conscious diners — observant Muslims, families, anyone keeping a strict diet — can search at **halalfoodnearme.com** to find restaurants where the menu posture, supplier, and certification have all been checked. Restaurants apply through our owner portal to be verified.

### Who's behind Trust Halal?

We're a Muslim-led, community-built team. The platform was started because most halal-conscious diners have a story about being told a place is halal and finding out it wasn't, and the information is too scattered across Yelp, Google, and group chats. We built Trust Halal to put it in one place and check it.

### Where is Trust Halal available?

We're growing one city at a time. Verified listings are live in [your launch city]. If your restaurant is outside that area, you can still apply — we add cities as we have verified restaurants in them.

### Is this connected to any specific mosque or religious organization?

No. We're independent. We respect the work that certifying bodies (IFANCA, HFSAA, HMS, local mosques) do, and we surface their certificates on the listings, but Trust Halal is its own thing.

## Verification — the process

### How long does verification take?

About 10–15 minutes of your time to fill out the application. Our review usually takes 3 business days, occasionally up to a week if we need to follow up on something.

### What do you actually verify?

Four areas:
1. **Menu posture** — fully halal, halal options on a mixed menu, separate kitchens, etc.
2. **Per-product meat sourcing** — for each meat you serve (chicken, beef, lamb, etc.): supplier name, slaughter method, where it comes from.
3. **Certification** — if you have a halal certificate from a recognized body (IFANCA, HFSAA, etc.), we capture it.
4. **Alcohol policy** — none, beer/wine only, full bar, alcohol used in cooking.

### Do I need a halal certificate to get verified?

No. We offer three tiers:

* **Self-attested** — you filled out the questionnaire honestly. No cert on file. Diners see a "Self-attested" badge.
* **Certificate on file** — you uploaded a current halal certificate from a recognized body. Diners can view and download it.
* **Trust Halal Verified** — you have a certificate on file AND a Trust Halal verifier has visited in person within the last 12 months.

All three tiers are visible on your listing — diners can filter by tier in their search.

### What does "Trust Halal Verified" mean specifically?

It means: (1) you filled out the questionnaire, (2) we have a current halal certificate on file from you, AND (3) one of our trained verifiers has visited your restaurant in person in the last 12 months. The in-person visit is the difference between this tier and "Certificate on file."

### How do verifier visits work?

Our verifier team is made up of vetted community members — typically food bloggers, mosque-affiliated reviewers, or active platform users. They visit unannounced, eat a meal, look at the kitchen if welcomed, and submit a report. They disclose any compensation (a comped meal, a paid partnership) so admin can weigh accordingly. You don't have to do anything — they don't need an appointment.

## Cost and business model

### How much does verification cost?

Verification is free. All three tiers, no hidden fees.

### How can it be free?

We're funded outside of restaurant fees in this phase. Long-term we'll likely add paid features (featured placement, premium analytics, etc.) but the verified listing itself will always be free. Charging restaurants to be honestly listed would defeat the point of the platform.

### Will I be charged later?

No — your verified listing stays free. If we ever add paid features in the future, they'd be opt-in additions, not requirements to keep your verified status.

## After verification

### What do I get when I'm approved?

* A public listing on halalfoodnearme.com showing everything we verified.
* A "Trust Halal Verified" badge for your physical window (we mail you a sticker) and digital channels (Instagram bio, website, email signature).
* Access to your owner portal where you can edit your listing, upload an updated cert, switch suppliers, etc.

### How do I update my listing?

Log into your owner portal at owner.trusthalal.org. Changes that affect the verified facts (supplier change, cert renewal, menu posture change) get re-reviewed within 48 hours; cosmetic edits (description, photos, hours) go live immediately.

### What if my cert expires?

Your owner portal flags it before expiry. Upload the renewal and you're back to "Certificate on file" — no re-application needed.

### Can I lose my verified status?

Yes, in two cases:
1. **You stop being verifiable** — e.g. you switch from a halal supplier to a non-halal one and update your listing accordingly. We change the tier or remove the badge depending on the change.
2. **A consumer dispute is upheld** — if multiple consumer reports come in saying your listing is inaccurate and our review confirms the inaccuracy, the badge comes off until the listing is corrected.

We always notify you and give you a chance to respond before any change to your status.

### How do consumer disputes work?

If a diner thinks your listing is wrong about something (e.g. they were told the chicken isn't actually zabihah), they can file a dispute on your listing. We route it to you first — you have a chance to correct or explain. If you and the consumer can't reconcile, our admin team reviews and decides. Most disputes resolve at the owner-reconciliation step.

## Privacy and what shows publicly

### What information is shown on my public listing?

* Restaurant name, address, phone, hours, cuisine.
* Menu posture and per-meat sourcing details.
* Certification status and (if uploaded) the certificate itself, viewable as a download.
* Alcohol policy.
* Your verified tier and badge.

### What's NOT public?

* Your supplier contact details (we capture the supplier name, but not phone numbers / contracts).
* Your private notes to our verification team.
* Your account email and login info.
* Any internal flags or admin notes.

### Can I delist my restaurant?

Yes, any time. From your owner portal there's a "Take my listing offline" option. We keep your data in case you want to come back; you can also request full deletion.

## Edge cases

### My restaurant is halal-friendly but not 100% halal. Can I be listed?

Yes. The menu posture field captures this honestly — "halal options on a mixed menu," "separate kitchens," etc. Diners filter for the level they personally accept. Being honest about a mixed-kitchen setup is more valuable than overclaiming.

### What if I don't speak English well?

The owner portal is in English for now, but we can do the application with you over a phone call in Urdu, Arabic, Bangla, or Turkish. Email hello@trusthalal.org with your preferred language and we'll set it up.

### My halal certificate is from a local mosque, not a national body. Does that count?

Yes. We accept certificates from any recognized halal authority, including local mosques. We don't favor any specific certifying body. The certificate is shown on your listing with the issuing authority named, so diners can decide what they trust.

### I run a food truck / ghost kitchen / multi-location chain. Does verification work for me?

Yes. Food trucks list as a single entity (with a "mobile" address that updates with your schedule, if you want). Ghost kitchens list each storefront brand separately. Chains can verify each location individually, or apply once and we'll work with you on a multi-location process.

### What about my drinks menu — does alcohol policy affect anything else?

No. Alcohol policy is a field on your listing so diners who care can filter for it. We don't penalize you or hide the listing for serving alcohol — we just want diners to know in advance.
