## Section 5 — How verification works

**Headline**
> How verification works

**4 steps, horizontal on desktop / vertical on mobile** (numbered circles in olive)

**1. Apply**
> Tell us your restaurant name, claim it, fill out the halal questionnaire. Takes 10–15 minutes.

**2. Upload your cert (if you have one)**
> Drag in your halal certificate PDF — IFANCA, HFSAA, local mosque, doesn't matter who issued it. If you don't have one, you can still get verified at the self-attested tier.

**3. We review**
> A real human on our verification team reviews your application — typically within 3 business days. We may follow up with a question or two; that's normal.

**4. You're live**
> Approved listings go live with the verified badge. We send you the digital badge assets immediately and ship the physical window sticker within a week.

## Copy notes

* No exclamation points anywhere on this page. The voice is confident, not enthusiastic.
* The word "verified" appears in roughly half the sections — that's intentional. It's the load-bearing concept.
* Headlines are written to stand alone if someone skims only the bold parts.
* The badge claim ("Get verified once, get answered for forever") is the page's hook line. Repeat it at top and bottom.
* No mention of competing platforms (Yelp, Google) by name. Allusion is fine; calling them out by name reads as defensive.
* The "free in this phase" framing is honest and sets expectations without committing to free forever.
