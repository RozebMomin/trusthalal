# Trust Halal — Marketing Package

This folder is the working set of marketing assets for Trust Halal and the consumer-facing brand **Halal Food Near Me** (halalfoodnearme.com). It's organized by audience and channel, with shared foundations (voice, visuals, pitch) at the top so everything downstream stays consistent.

## Strategic frame

Trust Halal is a two-sided marketplace. The owner side (halal-claiming restaurants) is small and targetable; the consumer side (halal-conscious diners) is large but diffuse. The highest-leverage early move is making owner onboarding feel valuable so verified owners become a distribution channel for the consumer side — every verified-window-sticker is a billboard, every owner who shares their badge on Instagram is a free impression.

**Current stage:** early traction. Goal for the next 1–3 months is the first 1,000 consumer users and a meaningful base of verified restaurants in at least one launch city.

**Primary audience:** Muslim community first — mosques, MSAs, halal-Twitter, Muslim food influencers, Muslim media. Copy is welcoming to non-Muslim halal-curious diners too, but the wedge is community credibility.

**Brand voice:** warm and community-focused. Family-friendly, mosque-safe, earnest. The vibe is "the trustworthy neighborhood halal guide your aunt would recommend." (See `style-guide/brand-voice.md` for the locked specifics.)

## How to use this

1. **Start with the foundations.** Read `style-guide/brand-voice.md` and `elevator-pitch.md` first. Everything else builds on those — if you change the voice, you'll want to redo the rest in light of the new voice.
2. **Pick a channel and ship.** Don't try to launch all four channels at once. The recommended order is: (a) owner outreach (highest leverage), (b) community partnerships in parallel (slow build, free distribution), (c) consumer social once you have 25+ verified restaurants in one city, (d) SEO content in the background (long-burn, do a couple per week).
3. **Personalize templates before sending.** Cold emails, mosque outreach, influencer DMs — all of these need to feel hand-written, not blasted. Use the templates as scaffolding, not as a copy-paste source.
4. **Track what works.** Even at this stage: a simple Google Sheet with one row per outreach attempt (date, who, channel, response, outcome) is enough. After 50 attempts you'll see patterns.

## What's NOT in here yet

A handful of things you'll want eventually but don't need on day one:

* **Real photography.** I've written a photo brief (`consumer/photo-style-guide.md`) but you'll need to actually shoot or source the images. See that file for stock-source and AI-tool recommendations.
* **Brand identity refinement.** I've codified the existing wordmark + olive-band aesthetic. A proper logo overhaul / brand refresh is its own project.
* **Paid ads.** No Google or Meta ad copy here — early stage, no budget assumed. When you're ready, the social post copy in `consumer/social-templates/` is a good starting point for ad copy too.
* **Long-form content (blog posts, guides).** The SEO plan in `consumer/seo-content-plan.md` covers the structure for these; the actual writing happens when you decide to invest in it.

## Notes on assumptions

* The verification offering is **free for restaurants** in this phase. All owner-facing copy reflects that. When you add paid tiers (e.g. featured placement), the sales sheet and FAQ will need updates.
* You're verifying **menu posture, sourcing, and certification** — not making any claim about the restaurant's prayer space, ambient music, etc. Copy stays focused on the food.
* "Trust Halal Verified" is the badge phrasing used throughout. If you decide to change the brand mark or badge wording later, find/replace is easy — but the design files are SVGs you'll need to update in parallel.
