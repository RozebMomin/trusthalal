# Elevator Pitch

Three versions, from shortest to longest. Use the one that fits the medium.

---

## One sentence (for a tagline, social bio, or a hallway answer)

> Trust Halal is a verified directory of halal restaurants — we check what's on the plate so diners don't have to call the kitchen first.

Alternates if the above doesn't fit:

> A directory of halal restaurants you can actually trust, verified by the community for the community.

> We verify halal restaurants — supplier, slaughter method, certificate on file — so finding a meal you can trust takes one search instead of three phone calls.

---

## One paragraph (for press, partnership emails, mosque outreach intro)

> Trust Halal is a verified directory of halal restaurants. Most halal-conscious diners have a story about being told a place is halal and finding out it wasn't — we built Trust Halal to fix that. Restaurants apply to be verified, we check their supplier, slaughter method, and certification, and the ones that pass earn the Trust Halal Verified badge. Consumers can search at halalfoodnearme.com, filter by what matters to them (zabihah only, no alcohol on premises, certificate on file), and trust that what they see is real. We're building the platform alongside the Muslim community, starting with restaurants in [your city] and growing one verified place at a time.

(Swap "[your city]" for the actual launch city once you've decided. Brooklyn? Detroit? Houston? Pick one for now — concrete beats generic.)

---

## Two paragraphs (for press releases, longer outreach, "About" pages)

> Trust Halal is a verified directory of halal restaurants. Most halal-conscious diners — observant Muslims, families raising halal kids, anyone who keeps a strict diet — have a story about being told a place is halal and finding out it wasn't. The information is scattered: Yelp claims, Google reviews, word of mouth from cousins, calls to the restaurant. Trust Halal pulls all of it into one place and verifies it.
>
> Restaurants apply to be verified through our owner portal. Our team checks the menu posture (fully halal? halal options only? shared kitchen?), supplier and slaughter method per meat, certification on file, and the alcohol policy. The ones that pass earn the Trust Halal Verified badge, which appears on their listing at halalfoodnearme.com and on a physical sticker for their window. Diners can search by city or cuisine, filter for the standard they personally keep (strict zabihah, certificate on file, no alcohol on premises), and report a place if something changes. We're community-built: Muslim-led, free for restaurants in this phase, and growing one verified place at a time.

---

## Notes on using these

* **One sentence** is your bio, your Twitter description, your sticker tag. The "verified" word is the load-bearing piece — don't drop it.
* **One paragraph** is what you paste into the first paragraph of a cold email or partnership pitch. Doesn't lead with the founder's story or the company's history; leads with the problem and the fix.
* **Two paragraphs** is for press, About pages, and any context where someone wants the "what + how" picture. Keep the first paragraph problem-focused and the second mechanism-focused.

Avoid in all versions:
* Founder backstory (save it for a separate "about the founder" piece)
* Specific numbers you can't back up (restaurant count, user count) until those numbers are big enough to brag about
* Any language that sounds like you're selling SaaS to enterprises ("platform" is fine, "solution" is not)
